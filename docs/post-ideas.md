# Blog Post Ideas

## Draft Candidates

### 1. "From Smart Factories to Humanoids: What 10 Years of Deploying AI on Real Machines Taught Me"
The opener. High-level narrative arc from factory AI → Tanzania → humanoid robotics. Establishes positioning. ~2500 words. No confidential details — all generalized lessons.

### 2. "The Three Gaps Between VLA Papers and Real Robots"
Practitioner analysis of published VLA work (OpenVLA, RT-2, π0, etc.) viewed through a deployment lens. What breaks when you leave the lab?

### 3. "Building My Own Jarvis: Why I Gave It Access to Claude Skills"
Personal-agent build log. Architecture, tool-use design, what surprised me. Ties individual builder work to the VLA agent design space conceptually.

### 4. "VLA at the Edge: What Changes When You Lose the Cloud"
Edge-inference for embodied AI. Why latency/privacy/cost matter. Current SoC landscape (Jetson Thor, Qualcomm, etc.). Connects my IoT + tiny-ML background to humanoid compute.

### 5. "Sound Is Underrated: A Short Case for Acoustic AI in Robotics"
Spin-off from my sound-based monitoring paper. Argues that microphones are the most underused sensor in current embodied AI stacks.

## Publishing Guidelines

- Weekly cadence (target)
- One substantive technical argument per post
- Minimum 1500 words for depth
- Never disclose confidential details from current employer
- Always connect research → real-world practice (the "gap" theme)
